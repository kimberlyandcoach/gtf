The storyline: A major new donor will be arriving at 2:00 PM for a consultation about supporting charities around Toledo. Your day starts off chaotic because your dog is sick, you’re at the vet at 8:00 AM, and you must take a meeting with your manager from the vet office using blurred background. After that, you get into the office and get set up for the donor meeting. (chat, meetings, files, channels/tabs, notifications, version history, team design, Loop, Planner, Whiteboard, and Copilot summaries).

Exercise Overview (90 minutes)

Audience: Teams users (beginner → advanced)

Format: Individual or pairs (recommended), with a “manager” role and “colleague” role

Outcome: By the end, learners will have:

  • Conducted a meeting with blur background, used core meeting controls, and shared content
  • Used chat features (mentions, reactions, formatting, file share, search) .
  • Demonstrated channel collaboration (posts vs replies, tabs) and chat vs channel decisions, plus pin/mute/notifications )
  • Collaborated on files with co-authoring, version history, and restore and created a simple content hub
  • Used Loop components and Planner (My Tasks/My Day + planner in channel)
  • Used Whiteboard and produced a Copilot-style summary (or manual fallback)

Roles (2–3 people works best)

  • You (GTCF Staff Member): Donor Relations/Philanthropic Services
  • Manager: Checks in at 8:00 AM, changes priorities, wants status updates
  • Colleague (optional): Programs/Grants coordinator who will help with prep

Trainer tip: If learners are solo, they can “role-play” by sending messages to themselves in chat, or you can provide a dummy colleague account.

Scenario Timeline & Tasks (Minute-by-minute)

0–5 min — “Your day starts wrong”

Story prompt: Your dog is sick. You’re at the vet and your manager pings you:

“We need a quick call now—big donor at 2 PM. I want a plan by 11 AM.”

Task A (Teams warm-up):

  1. Open Teams and locate your manager chat.
  2. Send a message using:
    • Formatting (bold a key phrase)
    • A reaction to a message
    • A mention (@Manager) in your reply .

Deliverable: A formatted chat message that includes @mention + reaction.

5–20 min — 8:00 AM “Vet Office Meeting” (Blur background + meeting controls)

Task B (Meeting fundamentals):

  1. Join a meeting (or create “Quick Check-in” and invite Manager).
  2. Turn on camera and apply Blur background (privacy at vet).
  3. Use at least two meeting controls:
    • Mute/unmute
    • Raise hand or reaction
    • Turn captions on (optional)
  4. Use meeting chat to post:
    • A quick status update (“At vet; will be at office by 9:15.”)
  5. Screen share for 60 seconds:
    • Share an “agenda note” (a OneNote page, Word doc, or even a blank document titled Donor 2PM Prep).

Deliverables:

  • Blur background used
  • Meeting chat message posted
  • Screen share performed

20–30 min — “Back at your desk” (Search + decision: Chat vs Channel)

Story prompt: You arrive at the office. You need to coordinate across staff without cluttering everyone’s inbox.

Task C (Search + chat vs channel):

  1. Search chat history for the manager’s donor note or “2 PM”
  2. Decide:
    • What belongs in channel (broad visibility, ongoing work)
    • What belongs in chat (private, quick coordination) .

Deliverable: Post a message in chat to your colleague:

“Let’s move donor prep to the Donor Engagement channel for visibility.”

30–40 min — Team & Channel Design (Reduce sprawl + naming conventions)

Story prompt: Your manager wants donor work centralized and reusable for future donors.

Task D (Design effective Teams): Create or plan a Team (or use an existing one) called something like:

“GTCF | Donor Engagement”

Add/plan channels with a naming convention, e.g.:

  • 01-Intake & Research
  • 02-Meeting Prep
  • 03-Follow-up & Commitments
  • Resources-Templates

Explain (in one channel post) how your naming reduces sprawl. .

Deliverable: A short channel post describing:

  • Team purpose
  • Channels and naming convention
  • How it prevents sprawl .

40–50 min — Channel Conversation Skills + Tabs

Task E (Channel conversations + tabs):

  1. In 02-Meeting Prep, create a new post with the subject:

“2 PM Donor Consultation – Prep Thread”

  1. Have a partner reply (not a new post) with one suggestion. .
  2. Add a tab to the channel:
    • A File tab (the prep doc), or
    • A relevant website tab (internal resource), depending on your environment.

Deliverables:

  • One post + one reply in the same thread
  • One channel tab added .

50–65 min — Files: Upload, Co-author, Version History, Restore + “Content Hub”

Story prompt: The donor will want options—fund types, local charities, and giving vehicles—so you need a clean prep packet.

Task F (File collaboration basics + intermediate):

  1. Upload a file to the channel’s Files (e.g., Donor-2PM-Prep.docx).
  2. Open it in Teams/desktop and co-author with a partner:
    • Add sections: “Donor Goals,” “Local Focus Areas,” “Suggested Charities,” “Next Steps.” .
  3. Demonstrate version history:
    • Make a deliberate change (“Delete a paragraph”)
    • Then use Version history to review and restore the prior version.
  4. Create a simple content hub in Resources-Templates:
    • Add a folder like “Donor Meeting Templates”
    • Move/copy the prep document there
    • Post a channel message linking to the file (“Single source of truth”)

Deliverables:

  • File uploaded and co-authored
  • Version history viewed + restore completed
  • “Content hub” folder created and referenced in a channel post

65–78 min — Planner + My Tasks/My Day + Loop component

Story prompt: Your manager wants tasks assigned and trackable before lunch.

Task G (Planner & meetings productivity):

  1. Add Planner as a channel tab in 02-Meeting Prep (or open Planner tied to the Team).
  2. Create tasks and assign:
    • “Confirm donor profile + interests”
    • “Compile 3 local charity recommendations”
    • “Print/prepare meeting packet”
    • “Draft follow-up email template”
  3. Open My Tasks / My Day and confirm at least one task appears. .

Task H (Loop components):

  1. In a chat with colleague or in channel conversation, insert a Loop component:
    • A checklist titled “2 PM Donor Meeting Checklist”
    • Add 5 checkboxes and have both people update it.

Deliverables:

  • Planner tab + 4 tasks created/assigned
  • Task visible in My Tasks/My Day
  • Loop checklist created and edited by participants

78–88 min — Whiteboard + Copilot-style Summary (or fallback)

Story prompt: You need a quick collaborative way to visualize donor goals and giving paths.

Task I (Whiteboard collaboration):

  1. Open Whiteboard from Teams (meeting or channel context).
  2. Create a simple board:
    • “Donor Priorities” (Education, Arts, Health, Neighborhoods)
    • “Giving Vehicles” (Donor-advised fund, scholarship fund, designated fund)
    • “Next Steps” (site visits, proposals, follow-up)

Task J (Copilot basics: summary):

  • If Copilot is available: generate a summary of your prep thread or meeting chat:
    • “Summarize key decisions”
    • “List action items and owners”
    • “Draft a follow-up message to manager”
  • If Copilot is not available: manually write a 5-bullet summary in the channel. (Still meets the learning intent.)

Deliverables:

  • Whiteboard created with 3 sections
  • Copilot summary (or manual equivalent) posted to channel

88–90 min — Wrap-up (Pin, Mute, Notifications)

Task K (Chat management + notifications):

  1. Pin the Donor Prep chat or channel.
  2. Mute a less relevant chat or adjust notifications for the Donor Engagement channel.

Deliverable: Show pinned item + describe what notification change you made.

“One of Everything” Checklist (Quick Grading Rubric)

Use this as your completion tracker (every item comes from the attached guides):

  • Chat: @mention, reaction, formatting, file/link share, search
  • Meetings: schedule/join, blur background, controls, meeting chat, screen share
  • Teams/Channels: team vs channel concept, posting vs replying, channel tab
  • Communication mgmt: chat vs channel decision, pin/mute, notifications
  • Files: channel vs chat files, co-authoring, version history + restore, file tab
  • Advanced structure: team/channel planning, naming conventions, reduce sprawl, content hub concept
  • Loop + Planner: Loop component + Planner in channel + My Tasks/My Day
  • Whiteboard + Copilot basics: whiteboard collaboration + summary/prompting
